Why SleepWiseReviews Exists

"Most 'best sleep product' lists are either sponsored or written by people who never looked past the box. We built the opposite - guides grounded in real research and honest reader feedback."

— Harry Soul, SleepWiseReviews

SleepWiseReviews began in 2020 with a simple goal: cut through sponsored fluff and marketing claims, and tell people which sleep products are actually worth their money - and which aren't. Too many "reviews" online are paid placements or written by people who clearly never looked past the marketing.

Every guide on this site is built the same way. We start with published sleep research, compare products against measurable specifications rather than vague marketing labels, and weigh in real feedback from readers and the people we work with. When a guide makes a clinical claim, it points to a credible source.

The goal is simple: help you spend money on things that actually work - and skip the ones that don't.

How We Build Our Medical-Condition Guides

Choosing a mattress with a medical condition is not the same as choosing one without one. A mattress that feels comfortable in the showroom can quietly worsen sciatica, trap heat that aggravates multiple sclerosis, or maintain pressure on plantar fascia just long enough to peak pain at first step.

Every condition-specific guide on this site is built around three pillars:

🩺
Clinical Mechanism
Each guide starts with the pathophysiology — what the condition does to sleep and how mattress properties interact with it (pressure thresholds, position requirements, thermal needs).
📊
Spec-Based Matching
Products are matched against measurable specs (ILD, coil count, zoned support, top-layer thickness) — not vague firmness labels that mean different things at different brands.
📚
Sourced to Research
Clinical claims reference peer-reviewed sleep research, condition-specific guidelines, and where relevant, registered medical authorities (CDC, NIH, NHS, AASM).
🚫
Not Medical Advice
Every condition-specific guide tells you to consult a clinician before changing sleep equipment that affects your condition. We help you ask better questions — we do not replace medical care.
Medical Content Notice. SleepWiseReviews provides educational sleep-product information, not medical advice. The guides on this site describe how mattress properties interact with sleep mechanisms for various conditions, based on published research and product testing. For any medical condition — including those covered in these guides — consult your physician, sleep specialist, or licensed clinician before changing your sleep equipment. Information here is intended to help you make informed product comparisons and discuss options with your clinician.
