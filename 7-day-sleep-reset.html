The Protocol

Seven Days. One Change Per Day.

Day 1

Audit Your Caffeine

Set a hard caffeine cutoff 10 hours before your target bedtime. Coffee's half-life is 5-6 hours — a 3pm latte is roughly 12% strength at midnight. Move the cutoff today. This one change typically drops sleep onset by 10-15 minutes within 3 days.

Day 2

Fix Your Wake Time

Pick one wake time and hold it for all 7 days — including weekends. Consistent wake time is the strongest zeitgeber (time cue) your body has. Variable wake times are the #1 cause of "social jet lag" and morning grogginess.

Day 3

Cool the Room

Target bedroom temperature: 65-68°F (18-20°C). Your core body temperature needs to drop 1-2°F to initiate and maintain sleep. A warm room prevents this drop. If AC isn't available, use a fan directed away from you — air movement matters, not direct cold.

Day 4

Create a Wind-Down Window

Designate 30-60 minutes before bed as "wind-down only." No work. No news. No problem-solving. Good activities: reading fiction, light stretching, shower or bath (the warmth followed by cooling accelerates the temperature drop). This trains your nervous system to recognize the signal.

Day 5

Address the Light

Turn off overhead lights in the evening — use lamps at eye level or below. Overhead light signals "midday" to your circadian system even at 9pm. Blue-light glasses help; no overhead light helps more. Dim your phone screen to minimum brightness after 8pm.

Day 6

Add Magnesium Glycinate Optional

200-400mg magnesium glycinate, 45 minutes before bed. Specifically glycinate — not oxide (4% bioavailability), not citrate (laxative at high doses). Magnesium activates GABA receptors and helps regulate evening cortisol. Give it 10-14 days to feel the full effect. Skip if you're on any medications — check with your doctor.

Day 7

Evaluate and Lock In

Look at your data. If you're tracking with an Oura, Fitbit, or even just subjective notes: what moved? Sleep onset, wake events, morning freshness. Pick the 2-3 changes that had the most impact. Make those permanent. The goal isn't a perfect 7-day protocol — it's identifying your personal high-leverage levers.

Most people find that 2-3 of these changes do 80% of the work. Keep those. The rest are optional.
