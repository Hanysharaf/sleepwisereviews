A mattress protector does two jobs: extends your mattress's life and preserves its warranty. Most mattress warranties are voided by staining — a single spill without protection can void a $2,000 mattress warranty. A $40 protector is among the highest-ROI bedding purchases you can make.

The challenge is finding one that protects without adding noise, heat, or a plastic-feeling surface. This guide ranks seven protectors across the waterproof, cooling, and encasement categories — with a clear breakdown of when each type is appropriate.

Quick picks: SafeRest Premium for the best waterproof performance without noise or heat. Purple Protector for sleepers with a Purple mattress or any hot sleeper. Lucid Encasement if bed bug protection is a priority.

Fitted vs Encasement: Which Type Do You Need?

Fitted protector (covers top + sides): For spill and sweat protection. Easier to use. Suitable for most sleepers. All 7 picks below except Lucid are fitted style.

Encasement (covers all 6 sides, zipper closure): Required for: bed bug prevention, severe dust mite allergies, or mattresses with existing contamination. The Lucid below is an encasement. More difficult to install but provides complete perimeter protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a waterproof mattress protector?

A waterproof mattress protector protects against sweat, spills, and bodily fluids that would create permanent stains and odors. Since most mattress warranties are voided by staining, a protector is effectively required to maintain warranty coverage. Especially recommended for children's beds, pet owners, heavy sweaters, anyone with incontinence, and anyone eating or drinking in bed.

What is the difference between a fitted protector and an encasement?

A fitted protector covers only the top and sides — easier to use, sufficient for spill and sweat protection. An encasement wraps all six sides with a zipper closure, providing complete protection against bed bugs and allergens on all surfaces. Encasements are recommended for bed bug prevention or severe dust mite allergies.

Will a waterproof mattress protector make my bed noisy or hot?

Older vinyl-based protectors were notorious for noise and heat. Modern protectors use TPU laminate rather than vinyl — TPU is quiet, breathable, and significantly more comfortable. High-quality TPU protectors like SafeRest or Coop are virtually silent and sleep noticeably cooler than vinyl. The noise and heat problems are largely solved with any reputable brand using TPU technology.

How often should I wash a mattress protector?

Every 1-2 months for normal use, or immediately after any spill. Most quality protectors are machine washable in warm water (not hot — heat degrades the waterproof membrane). Do not use bleach, fabric softener, or dryer sheets — these degrade the TPU layer. The waterproof membrane typically lasts 5-10 years with correct care.

Will a mattress protector affect mattress feel?

A well-designed thin protector adds minimal feel change — less than 0.5 inches between you and the mattress surface. Thin cotton-terry top protectors are the most transparent to feel. Thicker padded protectors marketed as adding cushioning will noticeably change the sleep surface and may work against the firmness level you chose in your mattress. For maximum feel transparency, choose a thin waterproof protector without extra padding.
