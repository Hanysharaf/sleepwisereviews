Bunk bed mattresses have one constraint that no other mattress purchase has: the guardrail safety height. The top bunk guardrail is designed to prevent falls — but it only works if the mattress is thin enough that the rail still extends at least 5 inches above the mattress surface. Buy a mattress too thick for the top bunk and you eliminate the guardrail's protection. This guide covers the 7 best mattresses for bunk beds in 2026, with a safety thickness guide, top vs. bottom bunk requirements, and weight limit information.

Safety First — Top Bunk Thickness Limit

The CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) requires that bunk bed guardrails extend at least 5 inches above the mattress surface. If your bunk bed's guardrail is 10 inches tall and you use an 8-inch mattress, the guardrail extends only 2 inches above the sleeper — not enough to prevent falls. For most standard bunk frames with 10-inch rails, use a maximum 6-inch mattress on the top bunk. Measure your specific frame before purchasing.

Top vs. Bottom Bunk — Different Requirements

Top Bunk Requirements
  • Maximum 6-8 inches thick (guardrail safety)
  • Lightweight preferred (easier sheet changes)
  • Firm to medium-firm (children need firm support)
  • Twin size in most standard frames
  • CertiPUR-US foam (no harmful chemicals near kids)
  • Waterproof cover recommended (accidents)
Bottom Bunk Requirements
  • No thickness limit (no guardrail constraint)
  • 8-12 inches for adult/teen comfort
  • More firmness options available
  • Can use standard twin or full in larger frames
  • Better motion isolation helps if top bunk moves at night
  • Higher-profile OK — easier to get in and out
