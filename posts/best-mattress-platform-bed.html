Frequently Asked Questions

Do platform beds need a box spring?
No. Platform beds are specifically designed to support a mattress directly without a box spring. Adding one will raise the bed height unnecessarily and may void mattress warranties. The solid or slatted base provides the required support.
What is the maximum slat spacing for a platform bed?
Most mattress manufacturers recommend slat spacing no wider than 2.75 to 3 inches (7 cm). Wider gaps cause foam to sag into the gaps over time, reducing support and shortening mattress life. For memory foam, 2 inches or less is ideal.
What mattress height is best for a platform bed?
Platform beds sit 6–12 inches lower than traditional frames. A mattress of 10–12 inches brings the sleep surface to a comfortable 18–24 inches total height. Very thick mattresses (14+ inches) can feel too high on a low-profile platform frame.
Can I use a spring mattress on a platform bed?
Yes, but check slat spacing first. Innerspring and hybrid mattresses work on slatted platforms with slats no more than 3 inches apart. Wider gaps can cause coils to shift or create pressure points. Solid platform surfaces work best with any spring mattress.
Will a mattress slide on a platform bed?
Some mattresses slide on smooth platform surfaces. Look for mattresses with non-slip covers or fabric-covered bases. Alternatively, a non-slip rug pad cut to size placed between the mattress and platform will prevent movement entirely.
