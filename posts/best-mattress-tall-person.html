How to Calculate the Length You Actually Need

The rule: your mattress should be at least 6 inches longer than your height to allow for natural movement, pillow positioning, and leg extension during deep sleep. Formula: Your height (inches) + 6 = minimum mattress length needed. Example: 6'4" = 76 inches + 6 = 82 inches minimum. A standard King (80") falls short. A California King (84") provides the 2-inch margin needed. Always measure yourself in sleeping position — many people gain 0.5 to 1 inch in length when lying flat due to spinal decompression.

Frequently Asked Questions

What mattress size do tall people need?

People up to 6'2" fit in standard 80-inch sizes (Queen, King). People 6'2" to 6'8" need a California King (84 inches). People over 6'8" require a custom-length mattress.

Is California King better than King for tall people?

California King adds 4 inches of length (84" vs 80") at the cost of 4 inches of width (72" vs 76"). For solo tall sleepers or tall couples where length is the priority, Cal King is better. For tall couples where width matters more, a King may be sufficient if you are under 6'3".

What firmness should tall people use?

Medium-firm (6-7 out of 10) works for most tall back sleepers. Tall side sleepers do better with medium (5-6) for hip and shoulder cushioning. Tall stomach sleepers need firm (7-8) to prevent hip sinkage.

Can tall people use a Queen mattress?

Up to 6'2", yes — with 6 inches of clearance. But if you stretch out during the night, your feet may hang off the edge. Above 6'2", invest in King or California King for reliable coverage during movement.

Do tall people need a thicker mattress?

Height alone does not require a thicker mattress. Weight distribution matters more. Tall heavy sleepers benefit from 12+ inch mattresses to avoid bottoming out. Tall lightweight sleepers do fine with standard 10-12 inch options.
