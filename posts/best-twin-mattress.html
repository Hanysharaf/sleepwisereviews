Twin vs Twin XL: Which Size Do You Need?

Twin
38" x 75"
Children under 5'6" / Bunk beds / Short-term guest rooms
Twin XL
38" x 80"
Teens, college dorms, adults under 6'2" / Long-term use
Full / Double
54" x 75"
Single adult who wants more width / Teen upgrading from twin

When to Choose Twin XL Over Twin

The extra 5 inches of length in Twin XL costs the same as Twin on most mattresses. If there is any chance your child will be taller than 5'6" as an adult — buy Twin XL. College dorms standardize on Twin XL, so buying Twin XL now means the mattress works for a dorm room in 5-10 years. The price difference is rarely more than $50-100.

Buying by Use Case

Child's Primary Bed (Ages 3-12)
Priority: Safety certs (GREENGUARD Gold), medium-firm (6-7/10), durable materials. Budget: $350-700.
Teenager's Bedroom
Priority: Medium firmness, Twin XL length, motion isolation. Budget: $500-900. Buy Twin XL.
College Dorm
Priority: Twin XL, lightweight for moving, budget-friendly, fits standard dorm frames. Budget: $200-500.
Bunk Bed
Priority: Low profile (8-10 inches max for top bunk safety), standard Twin size, firm. Budget: $200-500.
Guest Room (Occasional)
Priority: Medium firmness (versatile), good value, decent trial period. Budget: $300-600.
Small Studio Apartment
Priority: Low profile or narrow build, quality materials for daily use. Buy Twin XL. Budget: $400-800.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the dimensions of a twin mattress?

A standard twin mattress measures 38 inches wide by 75 inches long. This is 17 inches narrower and 5 inches shorter than a full/double mattress. Twin XL measures 38 x 80 inches — same width as twin but 5 inches longer, making it ideal for taller sleepers and college dorms.

Is a twin mattress big enough for an adult?

A twin mattress (38 x 75 inches) can accommodate an adult under 6 feet tall for solo sleeping. However, it offers very limited space to move. For adults, Twin XL (38 x 80 inches) is recommended. Twin is best suited for children under 12 and short-term guest use.

How much should I spend on a twin mattress?

Budget tier: $150-300 (Zinus, Linenspa) — basic foam, short lifespan. Mid-range: $300-700 (Tuft & Needle, Casper) — better materials, 7-10 year lifespan. Premium: $700-1500 (Saatva, Helix Luxe) — luxury materials, 10+ year lifespan. For a child's primary mattress, mid-range is the minimum.

Twin vs Twin XL: which should I buy?

Buy Twin (38x75) for children under 5'6" under age 12, bunk beds, or short-term guest rooms. Buy Twin XL (38x80) for teenagers or adults over 5'6", college dorms (standard dorm bed size), or if you want a mattress that lasts through the teenage years without upgrading.

What firmness is best for a twin mattress?

For children ages 3-12: medium-firm (6-7/10). For teens and adults: medium (5-6/10). For guest rooms used occasionally: medium (5-6/10) is most versatile. Avoid very soft twins unless for a specific therapeutic purpose — most twin sleepers need more support than a soft mattress provides.
