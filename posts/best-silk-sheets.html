Momme Weight Explained: The Only Number That Matters in Silk

Momme (mm) is the silk equivalent of GSM in cotton — it measures the weight of 100 yards of fabric that is 45 inches wide. A higher momme number means more silk filament per unit area, producing a heavier, more durable, and more opaque fabric. For sheets: 19mm is standard weight (light, breathable, affordable). 22mm is the premium sweet spot (durable enough for years, light enough to breathe). 25mm is heavyweight (maximum durability, slightly warmer, higher cost). Grade 6A silk (the highest commercial grade) indicates the longest, most uniform, most lustrous filaments — it matters more in lower momme weights where inferior filaments are more noticeable.

Silk Sheets Buying Guide

Silk vs. Satin: The Crucial Difference

Satin is a weave structure, not a material — polyester can be woven in a satin structure to look like silk. Many "silk feel" products on Amazon are polyester satin, not silk. True silk comes from silkworm cocoons. Check for explicit "mulberry silk" claims, momme weight specification, and OEKO-TEX certification. If momme weight is not listed, it is almost certainly not real silk.

OEKO-TEX: The Minimum Certification Standard

OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certification confirms no harmful substances in the finished product. For silk, this is especially important because silk dyeing processes can involve formaldehyde and heavy metals in unregulated manufacturing. Only buy OEKO-TEX certified silk sheets — it is the minimum acceptable standard, not a luxury feature.

Care: The Non-Negotiable Rules

Silk is protein-based and degrades with alkali, heat, and abrasion. Wash in cool water with pH-neutral silk detergent. Never use bleach, fabric softener, or hot water. Air dry away from sunlight (UV degrades silk). Never wring or twist. If machine washing: mesh bag, delicate cycle, cool water only. The 25mm options are more tolerant of machine washing; 19mm should be hand-washed.

Hair and Skin Benefits: Real or Marketing?

The claim that silk reduces hair breakage and pillow lines is real, not marketing. Silk's smooth surface produces significantly less friction than cotton (which is why cotton pillowcases are associated with hair damage and sleep creases). The amino acids in silk do not penetrate skin at typical contact durations, so skin-nourishing claims are not meaningful — but the reduced friction benefit is genuine.

Frequently Asked Questions

What momme weight is best for silk sheets?

For sheets, 19-25 momme is the ideal range. 19mm is lightweight and breathable. 22mm is the best all-around balance of durability and feel. 25mm is heavier, more durable, and the premium choice. Below 19mm wears quickly.

Is mulberry silk better than other silk?

Yes. Mulberry silk comes from silkworms fed exclusively on mulberry leaves, producing long, uniform, lustrous filaments. Wild silks have shorter, more irregular filaments and a rougher texture. For bed sheets, mulberry silk is the standard to look for.

How do you wash silk sheets?

Hand wash in cool water with a pH-neutral silk detergent, or use the delicate cycle in a mesh laundry bag. Never hot water, bleach, or fabric softener. Air dry away from direct sunlight. Never wring or twist silk.

Are silk sheets worth the price?

For buyers who prioritize feel, temperature regulation, and hair/skin benefits — yes. Quality silk sheets last 5-10 years with proper care, making the per-night cost competitive with premium cotton when calculated over their lifespan.

What is OEKO-TEX certification for silk?

OEKO-TEX Standard 100 confirms no harmful substances in the finished textile. For silk, this is critical because unregulated silk dyeing can use formaldehyde and heavy metals. Only buy OEKO-TEX certified silk sheets.
