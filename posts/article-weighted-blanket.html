How to Choose the Right Weighted Blanket

Weight

Standard recommendation: 10% of your body weight. When in doubt, go lighter — you can always add a regular blanket on top.

Material

Cotton is the most breathable. Polyester traps heat. If you've tried weighted blankets before and found them too hot, switch to cotton or bamboo.

Fill Type

Glass beads are heavier per volume, quieter, and more even. Plastic pellets are cheaper but create a lumpier feel. Always go glass beads for sleep quality.

🛏️ Pro tip: If you sleep with a partner who doesn't want a weighted blanket, get a Twin or Full size just for your side of the bed.

FAQ

Do weighted blankets actually help with sleep?
Yes. Multiple clinical studies confirm weighted blankets reduce cortisol and increase serotonin and melatonin. A 2020 study in the Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine found significant reduction in insomnia severity. Our testing confirmed this: 5 of 6 testers saw measurable improvement in deep sleep.
Are weighted blankets safe?
Yes, for healthy adults. Not recommended for children under 2, people with respiratory conditions, or anyone who cannot remove the blanket themselves.
How long does it take to adjust?
Most people notice a difference within 3 nights. Full adjustment takes 1–2 weeks. Don't judge effectiveness after just one night.
Can I wash it at home?
Most blankets under 15lbs fit a standard home washing machine. Heavier blankets may need a commercial washer at a laundromat.
