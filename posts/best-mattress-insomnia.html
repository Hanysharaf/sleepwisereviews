How Physical Comfort Affects Sleep Onset

The body must drop its core temperature by 1-2°F to initiate sleep. A 2019 study in Science Advances found that manipulating skin temperature through a cooling suit changed sleep onset time by an average of 18 minutes. A separate 2012 study in the Journal of Physiological Anthropology confirmed that pressure point discomfort in the lying position elevates cortisol, which directly opposes the melatonin-driven sleep cascade. Mattresses that address heat and pressure remove these two cortisol-elevating barriers without touching the psychological components of insomnia.

The 3 Physical Barriers a Mattress Can Address

1. Heat Retention

Delays sleep onset by opposing the body's natural core temperature drop. Solutions: GelFlex Grid, copper foam, open-coil airflow, breathable covers.

2. Pressure Points

Trigger cortisol micro-arousals that fragment sleep architecture. Solutions: zoned support, pressure-neutral surfaces, correct firmness for body weight.

3. Partner Motion

Physical disturbance events that trigger partial arousal. Solutions: individually wrapped coils, high-isolation foam layers, split designs.

What a Mattress Cannot Fix

Conditioned wakefulness, hyperarousal, sleep anxiety, racing thoughts. These require CBT-I treatment, not a product purchase.
