Nighttime acid reflux (GERD) disrupts sleep for roughly 25% of the US adult population. The root cause is gravity: lying flat removes the advantage of the upright position that keeps stomach acid below the esophagus during the day. The solution is head elevation — and that requires a mattress that works at an angle. We reviewed 7 mattresses for adjustable base compatibility, anti-slide surface, and off-gassing profile (chemical fumes worsen esophageal irritation).

The Evidence on Elevation

A 2006 systematic review in Archives of Internal Medicine confirmed that head-of-bed elevation reduces esophageal acid exposure in GERD patients. The recommended minimum is 6-8 inches above heart level. A 2012 study in Journal of Clinical Gastroenterology found 20 cm elevation reduced nocturnal acid exposure time by 67% compared to flat sleeping. Left-side sleeping combined with elevation produced the best outcomes of all position combinations tested.

Wedge Pillow vs. Adjustable Base

Wedge Pillow ($50–$150)

Fixed angle (typically 30-45 deg). Portable. Works with any mattress. Can cause neck strain at higher angles. Best for: budget-first, renters, mild GERD.

Adjustable Base ($500–$2,000+)

Variable angle 0-60 deg. Knee elevation prevents body slide. Split option for couples. Dual benefit: GERD + back pain + snoring. Best for: multi-issue users, long-term investment.
