Best RV Mattress 2026 — 7 Expert Picks for Short Queen, Full, and More

😴
By Harry Soul - SleepWiseReviews
Independent Sleep Researcher
RV mattresses fail on three fronts: wrong size (short queen vs. RV queen vs. standard), poor temperature performance in extreme conditions, and off-gassing that’s amplified in a small enclosed space. These 7 picks are chosen for the specific demands of RV life — compressed delivery that fits through RV doors, size options beyond standard dimensions, and materials that perform whether you’re parked in desert heat or mountain cold.
📌 Measure Your RV Platform Before Ordering RV bed sizes are not standardized. A "short queen" can be 60x72, 60x74, or 60x75 depending on the manufacturer and year. Before ordering any mattress, measure your RV platform in both length and width. Many RV mattress brands (Brentwood Home, Serenia Sleep) offer cut-to-size options — this is worth the small premium versus returning a mattress that's 2 inches too long.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size is a short queen RV mattress?
A short queen RV mattress is 60 inches wide by 72–75 inches long — compared to a standard queen at 60x80. The most common short queen dimension is 60x74. Always measure your RV bed platform before ordering, as dimensions vary by manufacturer.
Can I use a regular mattress in my RV?
Only if the dimensions match your RV platform. A standard queen (60x80) will fit in RVs with RV-queen platforms, but will not fit in short-queen platforms. Memory foam mattresses compressed in boxes can be maneuvered through RV doors more easily than innerspring mattresses.
What is the best mattress for full-time RV living?
For full-time RV living, latex or hybrid mattresses are best because they handle temperature extremes better than memory foam. Memory foam gets very firm in cold temperatures and very soft in heat — common conditions in an RV parked without hookups. A medium-firm latex or hybrid with a removable, washable cover is the top recommendation.
How do I get a new mattress into my RV?
Compressed foam and hybrid mattresses shipped in a roll come through RV doors without issue. Standard innerspring mattresses are rigid and typically cannot be maneuvered around RV corners. Leave the mattress compressed in the box until it’s at the bed platform, then unbox and allow 24–48 hours to fully expand before sleeping.
Do RV mattresses off-gas? Is it dangerous in a small space?
New foam mattresses do off-gas VOCs for the first 24–72 hours. In a small, enclosed RV with limited ventilation, this is more noticeable than in a house bedroom. Look for CertiPUR-US certified foam and ventilate the RV fully for at least 48 hours before sleeping on the new mattress. Natural latex off-gases significantly less than conventional foam.
