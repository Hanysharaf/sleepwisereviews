The Science of Deep Pressure Stimulation (DPS)

Weighted blankets work through a mechanism called Deep Pressure Stimulation (DPS) or Deep Pressure Touch (DPT). Here is what the research shows:

  • Autonomic nervous system: DPS activates the parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) branch of the autonomic nervous system, reducing fight-or-flight activation. Heart rate, respiratory rate, and blood pressure all decrease measurably.
  • Serotonin and dopamine: DPS stimulates mechanoreceptors in the skin that trigger serotonin production. Serotonin is a precursor to melatonin — the sleep hormone. This is one mechanism by which weighted blankets improve sleep onset.
  • Cortisol reduction: Studies show measurable reductions in cortisol (the primary stress hormone) after weighted blanket use. Lower cortisol enables deeper sleep stages.
  • Clinical evidence: A 2020 randomized controlled trial in the Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine found weighted blankets (with approximately 12% of body weight) significantly improved insomnia severity, fatigue, depression, and anxiety compared to light blankets.
  • Sensory processing: DPS is well-established in occupational therapy for individuals with sensory processing differences. The same mechanism that helps autistic individuals regulate sensory input also benefits neurotypical adults under stress.
  • The 10% rule: The 10% of body weight recommendation comes from OT practice, not rigorous clinical trials — but it has remained the standard because most people find it provides enough pressure without feeling restrictive.

Weighted Blanket Buying Guide

Hot Sleepers

Choose Bearaby Cotton Napper (open-knit breathes best) or Baloo (100% organic cotton, no polyester). Avoid microfiber shells or thick cover materials. Consider going 1-2 lbs lighter than the 10% recommendation for better airflow.

Anxiety Relief

Gravity Blanket and YnM are purpose-built for this. Higher weight (closer to 12-15% of body weight) often feels more therapeutic for anxiety. Focus on even bead distribution in a 7-layer grid construction.

Children

Never exceed 10% of child's body weight. Use poly pellets over glass beads for safety. SensaCalm or Mosaic for therapeutic/OT use. Never use on children under 2. Consult an OT for children with sensory needs.

Couples Sharing

Weighted blankets work best for individual use — they're meant to conform to one body. For couples, consider two separate lighter blankets rather than one heavy shared blanket. A 15 lb blanket for each partner beats a 25 lb shared blanket.

Washing Ease

Blankets under 15 lbs can go in a standard home washer. 15-25 lb blankets usually require a commercial washer (laundromat). Choose a blanket with a removable, washable cover if you want to wash frequently at home without hauling to a laundromat.

First-Time Buyer

Start with YnM (affordable, Amazon returns easy). Try for 2-4 weeks before evaluating — many people need an adjustment period. If you love it, upgrade to Bearaby or Gravity for a premium long-term option.

Frequently Asked Questions

How heavy should a weighted blanket be?

The standard recommendation is 10% of your body weight, plus 1-2 lbs. For a 150 lb person, that's a 17 lb blanket. However, this is a guideline — some people prefer lighter (7-8% of body weight) and some heavier. Children should use significantly lighter blankets. Never exceed 15% of body weight.

Do weighted blankets actually help with anxiety?

Research supports weighted blankets for anxiety reduction. Deep pressure stimulation (DPS) activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reduces cortisol, and increases serotonin and dopamine. Multiple studies show reduced anxiety scores after weighted blanket use, particularly for people with sensory processing differences, ADHD, and anxiety disorders.

Are weighted blankets good for hot sleepers?

Standard weighted blankets can sleep warm. If you run hot, look for cooling-specific options: Bearaby's open-knit cotton construction allows airflow, Baloo uses breathable cotton fill instead of glass beads. Avoid weighted blankets with polyester shells if you tend to sleep hot.

Can weighted blankets be used by children?

Weighted blankets are used therapeutically for children with autism, ADHD, or sensory processing disorders, often under occupational therapist guidance. The blanket should be no more than 10% of the child's body weight. Never use on children under 2 years old. Consult a pediatric OT or physician before use with young children.

How do I wash a weighted blanket?

Most weighted blankets can be machine washed in cold water on a gentle cycle. Use a front-loading washer for blankets over 12 lbs. Tumble dry low or air dry. Many blankets come with a removable duvet cover to make washing easier — wash the cover frequently and the inner blanket less often.
