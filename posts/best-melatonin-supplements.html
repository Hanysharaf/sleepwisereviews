    The Dose Problem Nobody Talks About

    Walk into any pharmacy and the standard melatonin dose is 5mg or 10mg. But Dr. W. Chris Winter, sleep physician and author of The Sleep Solution (2017), explains that studies consistently show a dose of 0.5mg is just as effective as 5mg for sleep onset — and lower doses produce far fewer next-day grogginess effects and cause less disruption to your body's own melatonin production.

    The reason high-dose melatonin supplements became standard has nothing to do with efficacy — it has to do with supplement regulations. As a supplement rather than a drug, melatonin manufacturers compete on perceived strength rather than optimal dosing. The result: most people are taking pharmacological doses of a hormone that their pineal gland produces in microgram quantities.

    💊 The real target dose: 0.3–1mg for sleep onset. 0.5mg is the clinical sweet spot supported by multiple meta-analyses. Extended-release formulations at 1–2mg are appropriate for sleep maintenance issues (waking in the night). Anything above 3mg provides no additional benefit for sleep and increases side-effect risk.

    When Melatonin Helps (and When It Doesn't)

    Melatonin is a timing signal, not a sedative. Understanding this distinction changes everything about how you use it:

    • It helps: Jet lag, shift work, resetting a delayed sleep phase (night owls trying to sleep earlier), occasional situational insomnia
    • It doesn't help: Chronic insomnia driven by anxiety, stress, or poor sleep hygiene — these need CBT-I or behavioural interventions, not a timing signal
    • It can make things worse: Taken at the wrong time (too early or too late relative to your desired sleep time), it shifts your clock in the wrong direction

    The correct timing: take melatonin 30–60 minutes before your desired sleep time. Not before your normal sleep time — before the time you want to shift your sleep to.

    Quick Dose Reference Guide

    Jet lag (eastward travel): 0.5mg taken at 10pm at your destination for 4 nights

    Delayed sleep phase (night owl reset): 0.5mg at 8–9pm, incrementally moving earlier

    Sleep maintenance (waking in the night): 1–2mg extended release at bedtime

    General sleep onset: 0.3–0.5mg, 30 minutes before bed

    Important Safety Notes

    Melatonin is generally considered safe for short-term use in adults. Key considerations:

    • Not for long-term nightly use: Chronic high-dose use may suppress your body's natural melatonin production. Use it as a tool for specific situations, not as a permanent sleep solution.
    • Interactions: Melatonin can interact with blood thinners, immunosuppressants, and some diabetes medications. Check with your doctor if you take any regular medications.
    • Pregnancy and breastfeeding: Insufficient safety data — avoid during pregnancy and nursing.
    • Timing is critical: Taking melatonin at the wrong circadian time can shift your sleep in the wrong direction. Always take it 30–60 minutes before your desired sleep time.
